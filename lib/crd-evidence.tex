\section{Executive Summary}
%==============================================================================

On \textbf{March 19, 2024}, Complainant Donald Ernest Gillson---a 100\% Permanently and Totally Disabled Veteran with service-connected brain injury and PTSD---submitted a lawful request for a service animal accommodation to Swords to Plowshares, a VA-funded veterans service organization in San Francisco.

\textbf{Within hours of that request}, Swords to Plowshares deployed an organization-wide email blocking policy (SMTP 550 hard reject) that prevented the Complainant from communicating with the organization via email.

This blockade has been in effect for \textbf{784 consecutive days} as of May 10, 2026.

\subsection{Key Events During Blockade Period}

\begin{itemize}[noitemsep]
    \item Complainant was discharged to \textbf{homelessness} on August 16, 2024
    \item An Adult Protective Services investigation was \textbf{closed with false justification} on March 13, 2026
    \item Complainant was subjected to a \textbf{false 5150 psychiatric hold} on April 1, 2026, which was \textbf{cleared by Travis Air Force Base}
    \item Complainant has received \textbf{zero responses} from any named respondent
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Three Key Evidence Points}

\textbf{1. The Communication Accommodation}\\
The VA has documented that Complainant uses an AI communication tool called ``NEWT'' as a disability accommodation (VA Note, April 6, 2026). By blocking Complainant's email communications, Respondents removed a medically-documented communication tool from a 100\% P\&T disabled veteran.

\textbf{2. The Contact Paradox}\\
On March 13, 2026, an APS Investigator closed an elder abuse investigation with the justification: ``Unable to connect with Veteran.'' However, Complainant has documentation of \textbf{approximately 150 emails} sent to that investigator's official email address. This justification is contradicted by documented evidence.

\textbf{3. The Federal Clearance}\\
On April 1, 2026, Complainant was subjected to a 5150 psychiatric hold. \textbf{Travis Air Force Base evaluated and cleared him}---finding no basis for the hold.

\subsection{Relief Sought}

\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item Finding of disability discrimination under ADA Title II
    \item Finding of disability discrimination under California FEHA
    \item Finding of discrimination by federal funding recipient under Section 504
    \item Finding of elder abuse / dependent adult neglect under W\&I Code 15610
    \item Finding of retaliation for protected accommodation request
    \item Enforcement action against named respondents
    \item Policy changes to prevent future communications blockades
    \item Compensatory damages for documented harm
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The Contradiction}

\textbf{The case was closed claiming inability to contact Complainant.}

\textbf{Complainant has documentation of approximately 150 emails sent to the investigator's official APS email address.}

This is a direct factual contradiction.

Either:
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item The investigator received the emails and chose not to respond, OR
    \item APS has an email blocking policy similar to Swords to Plowshares
\end{enumerate}

In either case, the justification for closing the case is contradicted by documented evidence.

\subsection{Legal Significance}

Under California Welfare \& Institutions Code Section 15630, APS investigators have an obligation to investigate reports of elder abuse and dependent adult neglect.

Closing a case with a justification contradicted by documented evidence raises serious questions about:
\begin{enumerate}[noitemsep]
    \item Whether mandated duties were performed
    \item Whether the case closure was made in good faith
    \item Whether a dependent adult abuse investigation was properly conducted
\end{enumerate}

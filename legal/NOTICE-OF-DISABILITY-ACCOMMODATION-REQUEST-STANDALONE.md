## III. LEGAL AUTHORITY FOR ACCOMMODATION REQUEST

### A. Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) — Title II
**28 C.F.R. §35.150 — Effective Communication and Accessibility**

> "A public entity shall take appropriate steps to ensure that communications with applicants, participants, and members of the public who are individuals with disabilities are as effective as communications with others."

**Application**: Digital filing is effective communication. Hand-filling forms is NOT effective for someone with Parkinsonism and TBI.

### B. California Code of Civil Procedure § 1010.6 — Electronic Filing
**CCP §1010.6(g) — Accommodation for Electronic Filing**

> "The judicial council shall adopt rules to ensure that electronic filing accommodates persons with disabilities, including those with mobility, visual, or hearing impairments."

**Application**: Parkinsonism (mobility impairment affecting fine motor control) qualifies for electronic filing accommodation.

### C. Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act
**29 U.S.C. §794**

> "No otherwise qualified individual with a disability shall, solely by reason of her or his disability, be excluded from the participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination under any program or activity receiving Federal financial assistance."

**Application**: Courts receiving federal funding cannot exclude Defendant from participation by requiring hand-signing (a manual dexterity task Parkinsonism prevents).

### D. California Constitution — Article I, §7
**Access to Courts**

> "The people have the right to petition the Government for redress of grievances."

**Application**: Requiring hand-signing of documents when Defendant has documented hand tremors obstructs access to the court system.

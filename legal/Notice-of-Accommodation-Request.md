## V. LEGAL BASIS FOR ACCOMMODATION

### A. Americans with Disabilities Act Title II (28 C.F.R. §35.130)

**28 C.F.R. §35.130(b)(1)(i)** requires:
> "No qualified individual with a disability shall, on the basis of disability, be excluded from participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination by a public entity."

**28 C.F.R. §35.150** mandates:
> "A public entity shall ensure that communications with applicants, participants, and members of the public are as effective as communications with others."

Electronic filing IS effective communication. Hand-filling IS ineffective for someone with Parkinson-related tremors.

### B. California Code of Civil Procedure §1010.6

**CCP §1010.6(a)** states:
> "Courts shall accept for filing any document submitted in compliance with the standards adopted by the Judicial Council and the applicable local court rules, whether submitted electronically or in paper form."

**CCP §1010.6(c)** requires:
> "The court must accept documents filed electronically and must ensure that the filing does not create undue hardship or delay for any party or the court."

**CCP §1010.6(i)** specifically addresses disability:
> "The court may not charge any fee for electronic filing except as provided in this section and applicable court rules."

San Francisco Superior Court ALREADY participates in CAUTION e-filing. The infrastructure exists. The system is operational.

### C. Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. §794)

Section 504 prohibits disability discrimination by recipients of federal funding. San Francisco Superior Court receives federal funding. The Court must ensure equal access.

Electronic filing = equal access for someone with hand tremors.

### D. California Government Code §11135

**Gov. Code §11135** (California's Unruh Act provision):
> "Except as otherwise provided by law, no person shall, on the basis of disability, be excluded from full participation in, be denied the benefits of, or be subjected to discrimination in any program or activity that is funded, in whole or in part, by the state."

Courts are state-funded. Disability discrimination = unlawful.
